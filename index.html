About false celling

"False ceiling, also known as dropped ceiling or suspended ceiling, is a secondary ceiling installed beneath the original ceiling of a room or home. Typically supported by metal frames, it creates the illusion of a lower ceiling height. False ceilings serve multiple purposes, including providing a smooth and even finish to conceal structural elements such as beams and uneven surfaces. They offer opportunities to enhance the room's aesthetics by integrating feature or ambient lighting. Additionally, false ceilings are utilized for soundproofing or acoustics, enclosing beams, concealing HVAC ducts, and accommodating electrical wiring and fixtures."

"Various types of products are utilized for false ceilings, tailored to meet the specific requirements of customers. These products find application in both residential and commercial spaces. False ceilings can be broadly classified into three categories – Boards, Grid Ceilings, and Cornices. Additionally, alternative acoustic materials like Wood Wool Board and PET Polyester boards can be employed for enhanced acoustic treatment."

Our services

Gypsum

Gypsum boards, also known as drywall or plasterboard, are versatile construction materials used for creating interior walls and ceilings. Lightweight and easy to install, gypsum boards offer flexibility in design and can be painted to match any décor.

Cement board

Cement boards are durable panels made from a blend of cement and reinforcing fibers. They are commonly used in wet areas such as bathrooms and kitchens as a substrate for tile installation, providing excellent moisture resistance and stability.

T Ceiling

T ceiling systems, also known as suspended ceilings, consist of a grid framework with panels inserted to create a secondary ceiling. They are popular for concealing wiring and piping while providing easy access for maintenance.

WPC Lauwers

WPC Lauwers, made from a blend of wood fibers and plastic polymers, offer the look of wood flooring with added durability and moisture resistance. Suitable for indoor and outdoor applications, they are commonly used for decking and flooring.

Dry Wall

Drywall, or plasterboard, is a lightweight panel used for interior walls and ceilings. It consists of gypsum plaster pressed between two sheets of paper and is known for its ease of installation and versatility.

Wiring

Wiring services involve the installation and maintenance of electrical wiring systems in buildings. This includes wiring for lighting, power distribution, telecommunications, and other electrical components, ensuring safe and reliable electrical infrastructure.

Painting

Painting services involve the application of paint to interior and exterior surfaces of buildings. Professional painters prepare surfaces, apply primers, and use various techniques to achieve desired finishes, enhancing both aesthetics and protection.
